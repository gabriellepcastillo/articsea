Artic sea ice hits second-lowest level on record

In 2021, Arctic sea ice shrank to its second-lowest extent in more 40 years of satellite measurements. On 15 September, ice covered just 3.74 million square kilometers of Artic waters at its annual summer minimum. In only one other year - 2012 - has the annual minimun Artic sea-ice cover dropped below 4 million kilometers
